Introduction & Definition

  • Non-inflammatory disorder of musculoskeletal system
  • Characterized by presence of painful points and alterations in mandibular movement
  • Originates from hyperirritible spots located within taut bands of skeletal muscle
  • Also known as MYOFASCIAL TRIGGER POINTS

Alternative Names:

  • TMJ Pain Dysfunction Syndrome
  • Costen's Syndrome
  • Facial Arthromyalgia
3/7

Etiology (Causes)

Multifactorial Origin:

  • Psychological or central etiology
  • Occlusal or peripheral etiology
  • Intrinsic joint disorders etiology

According to Okeson - Trauma Types:

  • Direct Trauma:
    • Direct blow to jaw
    • Opening mouth too wide or for too long a period
  • Indirect Trauma:
    • Whiplash-type injuries
    • Local infection leading to myositis
    • Occupational & repetitive strain injury
    • Sleep disturbance & nocturnal habits
    • Oral parafunctional muscle tension
4/7

Predisposing Factors

Structural Discrepancy of Masticatory System:

  • Systemic disease and infections
  • Neoplasia and orthopedic imbalances
  • Noxious habits such as bruxism and teeth clenching

Precipitating Factors:

  • Trauma
  • Stress
  • Iatrogenic factors
  • Idiopathic factors

MPDS (Myofascial Pain Dysfunction Syndrome)

Key Components:

  • Contracture
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Internal derangement
  • Degenerative arthritis
  • Altered chewing pattern
6/7

Clinical Features & Diagnosis

Negative Characteristics:

  • Absence of clinical, radiographic or biochemical evidence of changes in TMJ
  • Lack of tenderness in TMJ area when palpated via external auditory meatus

Clinical Examination:

  • Extra-oral Examination:
    • Palpation of TMJ
    • Palpation of muscles of mastication & neck muscle
  • Intra-oral Examination:
    • Check occlusion for premature contacts, crossbite
    • Assess reduced vertical dimension
    • High points or premature contact
    • Attrition, wear facets, missing teeth, mobile teeth

Radiologic Investigations:

  • Conventional: OPG, Transcranial projection, Transpharyngeal projection
  • Recent: CT, MRI, CBCT, Bone scanning

Treatment & Management

Additional Diagnostic Tools:

  • Electromyography
  • Sonography
  • Thermography
  • Mandibular tracking device
  • Vibration analysis

Treatment Approaches:

  • Pharmacological management
  • Occlusal splint therapy
  • Stress management techniques
  • Physical therapy and exercises
  • Behavioral modifications
  • Surgical options (in severe cases)

Key Takeaway

Multidisciplinary approach is essential for optimal treatment results
